Gardening Tips

Expert advice to help you maintain a healthy and beautiful garden.

Watering Wisdom

Water plants in the early morning to reduce evaporation. Most plants prefer deep, infrequent watering rather than frequent shallow sprinklings. Check soil moisture by inserting your finger about an inch deep - if it's dry, it's time to water.

Pruning Properly

Always use sharp, clean tools to make clean cuts. Prune just above a bud facing the direction you want new growth to develop. Remove dead or diseased branches first, then shape the plant as desired.

Soil Health

Healthy soil is the foundation of a thriving garden. Add organic matter like compost annually to improve soil structure and fertility. Test your soil every 2-3 years to monitor pH and nutrient levels.

Pest Control

Encourage beneficial insects by planting diverse species. Identify pests before treating - many can be controlled with organic methods like neem oil or insecticidal soap. Remove affected leaves promptly to prevent spread.

Seasonal Care

Adjust care with the seasons. Spring is for planting and fertilizing, summer for watering and pest control, fall for cleanup and bulb planting, winter for planning and protecting sensitive plants from cold.

Plant Selection

Choose plants suited to your specific conditions - sunlight, soil type, and climate. Native plants often require less maintenance and support local wildlife. Consider mature size to avoid overcrowding.
